Jason Derulo Net Worth: Music Career &amp; Houses

American Singer, Songwriter, and dancer Jason Joel Desroleaux known by his stage name Jason Derulo net worth is $16 million.

With several Platinum-certified songs such as “Talk Dirty,” “Wiggle,” “Trumpets,” “In My Head,” “Ridin Solo,” and “Want to Want Me,” Jason has sold over 30 million singles since he began his career in 2009.

Derulo has been writing g songs for some A-lister rappers and singers, including P.Diddy, Lil Wayne, Sean Kingston, just to name a few. He always wanted to become a solo performer.

Derulo signed to record label Beluga Heights, and soon it became the Warner Music Group partner. Jason started recording and released his first single in May 2009, “Whatcha Say.”

The song sold over five million digital downloads and was certified triple platinum by RIAA certification. The song also reached number 1 in New Zealand as well as in the United States.

Similarly, with the success of the first single, Derulo got hyped and released his second single, “In My Head,” in December 2009, just like the first one, this single was certifies as double platinum and sold over 3.17 million digital copies.

After releasing few singles and gaining success, Derulo started releasing studio albums. His debut studio alum was “Jason Derulo” on March 2nd, 2010.

Album after album and back-to-back success, Derulo released his third US album on April 15th, 2014, named “Talk Dirty,” which surpassed all of his record sales previously. The album included two top 10 Platinum singles.

Derulo released his single “Savage Love” in June 2020, teaming up with producer Jawsh 685.

The song went so viral that kpop band BTS remixed the song. The remixed version of the song reached number one in the United States.

Early Life

Jason Derulo was born on September the 21st, 1989, in Miramar, Florida, USA. His parents were Haitian. He started writing and composing at a very young age and wrote his first song by the age of eight.

He started getting attention when he wrote a song for Birdman, who is a rapper, and even made a guest appearance in the song “Bossy.”

Jason Derulo graduated from Dillard High School in Fort Lauderdale.
